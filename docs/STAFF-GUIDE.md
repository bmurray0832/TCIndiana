# TC Indiana CRM — Staff Quick-Start

A 5-minute guide for center staff. The whole system runs on one habit:
**log every contact**. Everything else — alerts, the follow-up queue,
the dashboard, the reports — updates automatically from that.

## Signing in

Go to the CRM URL and click **Sign in**. Use the email address your
admin set up for you. If you see "access pending," your account hasn't
been created yet — ask your admin to add you in Settings → Users.

## The morning routine (5 minutes)

1. Open **Follow-Ups**. This is your to-do list, sorted by urgency.
2. Work from the top. Each row has an action button that opens the
   Log Contact form pre-filled for that person.
3. If today isn't the right day for someone, **Snooze** them — they'll
   come back when the snooze expires. Don't let them sit red.

## What the colors mean

| Color | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Green | Recently contacted — no action needed |
| Yellow | Getting stale — contact soon |
| Orange | Overdue — prioritize this week |
| Red | Seriously overdue — contact today |

Donors and prospects run on different clocks (a prospect goes red in
weeks; a donor in months). Logging any contact resets the clock to
green instantly.

## Logging a contact

From any person's profile (or straight from the Follow-Up queue):
**Log Contact** → pick the type (call, email, meeting…), the outcome,
add a note a teammate could pick up from, and — if you promised a next
step — set the follow-up date. That date puts them back in the queue
on the right day.

## Recording a gift

On the person's profile: **Add Donation** → amount, date, payment
method, campaign. If the person was a prospect, recording their first
gift automatically converts them to a donor — there is no separate
"convert" button. Tick the receipt/thank-you boxes as you send them.

## Adding someone new

**Prospects → New prospect** (or Donors → New donor). Name and one way
to reach them is enough to start; set the interest level (Hot/Warm/
Cold) so they land in the right place in the pipeline. The **Next
step** field is your note-to-self about what happens next.

## Sending email

From a profile: **Send email**. If you've connected your Outlook
account (Settings → Email integrations → Connect), the email goes out
from *your* address and replies land in your inbox. Either way, the
email is logged as a contact automatically — no double entry.

## Where to look things up

- **Prospects / Donors** — searchable, filterable lists. Click any
  column header to sort; click the cards at the top to filter.
- **Contact Log** — every touch, newest first, across the whole center.
- **Donations** — every gift with receipt/thank-you status.
- **Reports** — Monthly Report for the numbers meeting, Biggest
  Supporters before a board meeting, Retention and Funnel for the
  bigger picture.

## Weekly digest

Every Monday morning you'll get an email listing the people who most
need a touch from you, with links straight to their profiles. That
email is built from the same alert colors above — keep logging
contacts and it gets shorter.

## Three rules that make the whole thing work

1. **If you talked to them, log it** — even a voicemail.
2. **Always set a next follow-up date** when there's a next step.
3. **Never track someone in a side spreadsheet** — if they matter,
   they go in the CRM where the whole team can see them.
